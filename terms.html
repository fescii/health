1. Introduction

Welcome to Diasync Health, a digital health services platform connecting patients in Kenya with licensed Kenyan healthcare providers. Relatives or other financially responsible subscribers located in the United States may manage and pay for services on behalf of patients.

These Terms and Conditions ("Terms") govern access to and use of our services. By using the Portal, you agree to these Terms.

2. Definitions

  • Portal – Diasync Health, including websites, mobile applications, and related services.
  • Patient – the individual in Kenya receiving medical care.
  • Subscriber – the financially responsible person (often located in the U.S.) who pays for services on behalf of the Patient.
  • Provider – Kenyan-licensed healthcare professionals or facilities delivering services through the Portal.
  • Ambulance Provider – licensed emergency or ambulance service operators accessible via the Portal.
  • User – any Patient or Subscriber using the Portal.
  • We/Us/Our – Diasync Health, the platform operator.

3. Eligibility

  • Patients must be residents of Kenya and treated by Kenyan-licensed providers.
  • Subscribers must be at least 18 years old and capable of entering into legally binding agreements.
  • Users must provide complete, accurate, and current personal, medical, and payment information.
  • The Portal is not a substitute for emergency hotlines. Patients must always call Kenyan emergency services (e.g., 999 or 112) in urgent cases.

4. Patient Rights and Responsibilities

Patient Rights

Patients have the right to:

  1. Be treated with dignity, respect, and without discrimination of any kind.
  2. Receive safe, high-quality medical care from licensed professionals.
  3. Privacy and confidentiality of consultations, records, and treatments.
  4. Receive clear information about diagnoses, treatment options, risks, and benefits, in language they can understand.
  5. Give or withhold informed consent for treatments or procedures.
  6. Participate in care decisions, including seeking second opinions.
  7. Access their own medical records.
  8. Decide whether to grant the Subscriber access to their medical records.
    • Patients may grant or revoke Subscriber access at any time.
    • Declining Subscriber access will not affect the Subscriber's ability to pay for services.
  9. Receive an itemized bill and explanation of charges.
  10. Provide feedback or lodge complaints and have them addressed promptly.
  11. Have personal values, cultural beliefs, and spiritual needs respected.

Patient Responsibilities

Patients must:

  • Provide accurate medical and personal information.
  • Ask questions when information is unclear and follow agreed treatment plans.
  • Keep or cancel appointments in a timely manner.
  • Treat staff, providers, and other patients respectfully.
  • Protect login credentials and report unauthorized use.
  • Coordinate with Subscribers to ensure payment arrangements are met.

5. Portal Services

  • Appointment scheduling and reminders.
  • Secure messaging for non-urgent communication with providers.
  • Subscription and payment management for Subscribers.
  • Facilitation of ambulance booking for emergency evacuation either by road or air.

6. Account and Access
